On our second day, we visited Mengjia Longshan Temple (艋舺龍山寺), the oldest one in Taipei and National Chiang Kai-shek Memorial Hall (中正紀念堂) where you can see the famous change of guards ceremony. Then, we headed down to Taichung City (台中) where I was born and brought up until nine.

二日目では台北で最も歴史のあるお寺龍山寺、そして中正紀念堂では儀仗隊交代式を見学し、自分の生まれ育った町、台中へ南下する。

Day Two – Taipei & Taichung

We both woke up early in the morning and took a walk around the hostel and hoped to find a place to have traditional Taiwanese breakfast. The whole Ximen city was very quiet in the morning. Nobody was on the street, compared to the overflowing number of people in the night.

All shops were also closed.

We ended up finding a well-known breakfast place online and since it takes only 5 mins from Ximen MRT station by train, so decided to head towards the station.

I could not believe just having a breakfast took up so much of our time.

On the way to the train station, we finally found a breakfast place opened.

The plan changed, we decided to have a meal here, called 永和豆漿.

We ordered a traditional Taiwanese breakfast, which includes Fried bread stick (油條), Steamed buns (饅頭), Egg cakes (蛋餅), and cold Soybean milk (豆乳). Because all menu will be cooked immediately after being ordered, they are fresh and taste so good. We just kept ordering and ordering, and left the shop with full stomachs.

After breakfast, we returned to our place of accommodation and packed the luggage. It is time to say goodbye to Meander Hostel.

Mengjia Longshan Temple

Mengjia Longshan Temple (艋舺龍山寺) is our first destination for the day, it is located around 20 minutes walk from the hostel where we stayed.

Because it was on the weekend, the precincts are flooded with people. We bought a bunch of incense, and followed the crowds to worship each god, I think it was about 8 of them.

This temple was built in 1738 and is one of the oldest buildings that represent Taiwan. It is colorful, energetic and full of exotic atmosphere.

Taipei residents have consistently rebuilt and renovated it till just after the end of the Second World War. Longshan is seen as an emblematic example of Taiwanese classical architecture, with southern Chinese influences commonly seen in older buildings.

Mengjia Longshan Temple, Wikipedia

National Chiang Kai-shek Memorial Hall

From Mengjia Longshan Temple, we took our first taxi to National Chiang Kai-shek Memorial Hall National Chiang Kai-shek Memorial Hall (國立中正紀念堂). This is a side story but I always had an impression that the taxi drivers in Taiwan love to chat with customers about politics. However, our taxi driver did not speak a single word.

There are resting places, ponds and gardens within the memorial park. When you walk into the park, you will see some people doing exercises. I believe they try to make parks a part of recreation for residents. At the same time, it brought back memories of how many middle-aged or elderly women and some men gathering together and dancing in the morning being a common sight in the parks of Taiwan.

The Temperature rose, becoming hot, humid and sticky. It was typical summer in Taiwan. HeySong Sarsaparilla (黒松沙士) is one of most popular soft drink in Taiwan. Some people describe it as "Taiwanese Coca Cola". I feel it tastes like salty cider but really like its taste.

The nostalgic taste of Taiwan.

At Kai-shek Memorial Hall, changing of the honour guards ceremony is a well-known tourist attraction. There is a bronze statue of Chiang Kai-shek on the main floor, guarded by soldiers called honour guards standing on both sides during the day. Because it is so hot in the hall, there is a member staff to wipe the sweat off the honour guard at regular intervals.

The honour guard changes shift and perform drills each hour from 9am to 5pm. The drill takes about 15 minutes.

According to my mother, these guards are the elite of the elite amongst the armed forces including army, navy and air force.

I think I have been to this hall only once or twice in the past and this is my first time to actually see the ceremony.

Yongkang Street, Dongmen

After the memorial hall, we caught a train from National Chiang Kai-shek Memorial's MRT station to Dongmen, where you can visit Yongkang Street (永康街), an excellent walking street with good food, drinks (tea+juice kind), desserts and boutiques.

Once you are out of Dongmen train station, you will see many of shaved ice shops here and there. Their ice is a bit different from the normal shaved ice made by shaving a block of ice. It is called 'The snow ice', which is very fluffy like cotton snow, smoothly disintegrating in the mouth faster than cotton candy.

Perhaps I normally eat much sweeter in Australia, so it did not taste as sweet as I expected it to be.

We enjoyed our lunch in a small restaurant with Braised pork on rice (魯肉飯), Fried-Spanish Mackerel Thick Soup (𩵚魠魚羹), Meatball Soup (貢丸湯), Chinese water spinach (空芯菜) and our favourite Ba-wan (肉圓).

After a stroll around the street for coffee, we headed back to Taipei station.

Taichung

In the afternoon, we headed to Taichung (台中) by using Taiwan High Speed ​​Rail, where I was born. Because there is not much price difference between Standard and Business class on the tickets, we decided to purchase a business class ticket to experience the luxury.

This comfortable business class seat just made my one hour journey seem like a five-minute quick train trip. It also came with coffee and dessert. The comfort and excellent customer service are much better than my flight from Perth to Taiwan.

The Taichung station of Taiwan High Speed ​​Rail is separated from the main Taichung station, which is located three stations away. We had to transit the local line and headed to the main station. In the station, I found a tiny toy shop and decided to buy LEGO toys for my two little boys.

Taichung Station

The main Taichung station (台中車站) is full of people, cars and buses. It was just chaos.

Our accommodation in Taichung was at VIP Hotel Taichung which is a business hotel. It takes about ten minutes walk from the train station. The room is clean, good sized with two comfortable single beds.

There is a well-known Ba-wan (肉圓) restaurant in Taichung called Taichung Ba-wan (台中肉圓). This is the place my mother suggested to go if you like Ba-wan. I checked the location on Google Maps and it is located on the other side of the train station and not too far away from where we were. It probably takes 20 minutes walk from the hotel.

We took a quick shower and headed out to find this place.

Oh my God, we ended spending nearly 2 hours walking around the city, took tea breaks, checked the map again and again. When we finally arrived, the restaurant had a ‘holiday closed’ notice.

Apparently, it was closed due to Ghost Festival (中元節). During this festival, people pray, burn some stuff, offer food and drinks on tables just before the gates of hell close, to make sure all the ghosts are happy and prosperous.

While searching for the restaurant, we wandered off into the festival preparation.

At night, we had a quick dinner at a street stall near the hotel. As usual, we ordered Noodle Soup (陽春麺) and Ba-wan (肉圓). My brother bought more instant noodles for his bedtime snack.
