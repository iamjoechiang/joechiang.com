This is a photo story of my Taiwan trip in August 2014, with my younger brother. I had been wanting to travel back to Taiwan where I was born and raised. Perhaps I am getting older, but I miss my hometown sometimes.

It is really exciting to travel with my brother together for the first time. Our itinerary is to travel from Taipei to Tainan in 8 days (yes, I know it is too short).

A big thank you to my understanding family, our journey just begins.

Day Zero – Perth ✈ Taipei

It is 0:05AM in Perth.

My flight is just about to take off to Taiwan via Singapore, and in Taipei I will join my brother who left the day before from Tokyo, Japan.

Hopefully I can catch up with him in Taipei on time and safely. My whole family seems to worry about us. :)

The temperature in Taipei is around 32 degrees Celsius (°C) and the humidity is high. It will be a challenge to head to the summer of the Northern Hemisphere from the Southern Hemisphere winter.

I arrived in Taoyuan International Airport around 12:15PM on time. The first thing on my to-do list is to buy a pre-paid SIM card from one of the telecom service centres.

I bought the 10 day pass from Taiwan Mobile for NT$800, which includes unlimited 3G data usage and NT$550 worth of airtime. The shop was just next to the arrival gate and very easy to locate. The staff member was friendly and helpful. She replaced the SIM card and even tested the network for me.

Jumping on the shuttle bus to Kuo-Kuang from the airport, got me to Taipei Railway Station in just less than an hour.

Day One – Jiufen and Ximen

Wi-Fi is available free of charge on most major train stations in Taiwan so I could get in touch with my brother easily and it did not take us long to reunite at all.

There was a food court and small souvenir shops on the second floor of the station. We quickly had a light lunch and headed by train to Ruifang (瑞芳) station. We would get to our first destination, Jiufen (九份) from there by bus.

Jiufen was an old and declined gold mining town until the 1970s. But because of the success of the film A City of Sadness,, it is now one of the world’s tourism destinations in Taiwan.

People come to Jiufen to walk along Jiufen Old Street – a famous street full of gift shops and restaurants and plenty of tourists taking photographs. It's one of the best places to see how Jiufen has maintained the unique atmosphere of a mountain city.

The town was flooded with tourists, food stalls, restaurants, souvenir shops, arts, crafts and teahouses. Of course, a variety of shops line the streets and there are plenty of things to explore.

The local snacks all looked delicious and interesting to try though we only tried the Chinese Sausages (香腸) and the tea.

It is said that the Japanese anime Spirited Away creators drew inspiration for their spirit world from the streets of Jiufen.

The most famous landmark in Jiufen, Jiu Fen A Mei (阿妹茶樓) is the 100-year old teahouse. It is the inspiration behind the Bath House in the movie.

This is the outlook of the old stall inside the theatre which sells drinks and tidbits. You can also see an old cinema projector that is on display. These days, the venue is used for concerts and other similar events.

After a long day walking and shopping, we went into one of the teahouses and ordered a cold brewed Jasmine tea. This teahouse is perched on top of the hill and the panoramic view that you can see from the mountain cliff is breathtaking.

It would be a fantastic view at night.

Because it was Saturday, the whole town was packed with tourists. There was a long bus line for the return bus to the station. It would take another couple of hours until the night fell.

So we decided to leave and go to our first spot of accommodation, the Meander Hostel.

Ximen

This was the ticket (token) for Metro Taipei. To use it, you simply placed the token over the sensor and went through the gate.

I had never seen the use of a plastic token as a ticket. This must be environmental friendly, I like it.

The Meander Hostel is conveniently located, 15 minutes away from MRT Ximen (西門)

On the way to the hostel, you could see The Red House (西門紅), a Western-style red-brick octagonal structure built in 1908, which was Taiwan's first public market and the most well preserved Class III historical site.

The hostel ground floor had an open atmosphere with kitchen, tables, foosball and so on.

We booked a Double Ensuite room (approx. $NT 2,500 per night): the room was a bit small but was very clean and quite comfortable. However, it was a confined space for two 185cm (six foot inch) males sleeping together in a single double bed.

After a quick shower, we went out the town for the dinner. We had Noodle Soup (陽春麺), Tonkotsu Ramen (豚骨ラーメン), Japanese Style Jiaozi (ぎょうざ), and my brother's favourite Beef Noodle Soup (牛肉麺).

After dinner, we entered a cafe called Fong Da Coffee, founded in 1956, which was great. It had a really vintage feel inside and you stepped back in time about 50 years when you walked in the door. The Taiwan Coffee I ordered was smooth and delicious.

You must visit this place if you love coffee!
